Glitch Art's Rhetorike:
Human|Object Relations as Rhetorical|Digital Craft
What do I mean by this?
I mean: some glitch artists are rhetorical
their craft/techne/glitch art reveals the digital medium
as a call for us to establish a deeper relationship w/ the digital medium
But. This must be done through doing glitch art, not just experiencing the output of glitch art.
(1)Hacking source code texts, (2)Revealing procedural claims in such texts, (3)Experiencing such glitches
This is their Rhetorike.
Looking @ Nick Briz's Glitch Codec Tutorial
Operates rhetorically on 2 levels:
(1)Epideictic Text(s) & (2)Alter Ontological Position/Perception
Focusing on (2)Ontological Position/Perception
mainClaim: Through Briz's craft, he attempts to persuade others to engage in such a craft as well to "establish a relationship with the digital medium"
WHY?
For Briz: To reveal the politics coded into the digital medium
To take part in this rhetorical argument, you must respond to Briz via hacking and re-writing source code texts. One must become or have qualities of the glitch artist.
So, what is glitch art?
_____
Broadly speaking: it's "destructive generativity" (Menkman, Glitch Manifesto, 2010) with a focus on the breaking of "procedural flows"
"The glitch is a powerful interruption that shifts an object away from its flow and ordinary discourse, towards the ruins of destructed meaning. This concept of flow I emphasize as both a trait within the machine as well as a feature of society as a whole." (Menkman, The Glitch Moment(um) 2011, 29)
Emphasis on processes and craft/techne, or how the means /are/ the end, the aim, the goal.
Use of digital materials. Some break hardware through circuitbending; many hack source code texts in a process called databending.
Briz, in particular, uses glitch art to also expose the politics coded into the digital medium.
Let's do an example. Glitch Art -- Databending 101
That's with image files. We're looking at video files.
Example output: Briz's "A New Ecology for a Citizen of the Digital Age"
Briz frames his work through a Media Studies lens: McLuhan.
Also, through Heidegger's "enframing" to reveal the relationship between the human and tool.
Specifically, Briz reveals the procedural claims operating via the digital medium
Programs make claims about how both person and machine should operate in a given context.
Lawrence Lessig argues "code is law" (1999); a form of "social control"
Wendy Chun extends the reach of Lessig's claims: Software is code, code is law, but code is also "executable" (2008).
For Chun: Code automatically gives/takes away human/machine operations.
In Rhet/Comp: Selfe & Selfe's application of Pratt's "contact zones" in "Politics of the Interface"
Briz claims that doing glitch art reveals the "laws" of the digital environment
McLuhan calls this the production of the "anti-environment"
Briz's Glitch Codec Tutorial creates this "anti-environment" writes new..,
laws
definitions of space and place
via the crafting and execution of a new digital environment to inhabit and understand and perceive in a new way
A fish out of water, crafts a new pond
But what are the politics of video codecs?
video codecs: code/encode video files via compression algorithms
Patents on video codecs: Apple's H.264 vs. Google's WebM
Briz's tutorial hacks/glitches the FFMPEG video codec library
Depending on your location, this is illegal
From the onset, a new glitch artist is embodying themselves in this rhetorical situation, as they craft this video codec library.
They re-write the environment, where the source code text becomes the "contact zone"
New procedural claims are constructed through the destruction of the original text.
New laws, new executables.
  • Create new linux distribution
  • Create new FFMPEG library
  • a new environment
What is Briz's Rhetorike?
Rhetorical arguments are made via source code executed as software environments and operations.
To become a glitch artist is to alter one's role in this situation.
Through this craft, one alters their ontological position from user to creator
This change alters one's understanding and relationship with the digital medium
Craft becomes central to Briz's rhetorical success, as well as necessary to take part in this discourse.
